The Digital Divide

Economic Factor


The economic factor can be taken as a main factor underlying the digital divide. Because a lot of money needed to purchase computers and peripheral devices. Most of the people in some developing countries cannot afford those facilities. A country like Africa has a low income so people who live there trying to fulfil basic things needed to live such as clean water, food, health care, etc.

Locally

Sri Lanka is a developing country with 6 million internet users out of 20.9 million countries’ population. Internet cost is very high in Sri, therefore, most of the people in this country use their data packs carefully. Some places in this county don’t even have electricity as a result people who live there are not familiar with digital devices. However, most of the urban areas have high-speed 4g internet coverage this way internet service providers earn a high amount of money from the urban areas in this country.

Sri Lankan government trying to reduce the gap by providing free Wi-Fi in public places, tabs for the government school children and making new ICT labs. Most of the old people in rural areas in Sri Lankan are non-educated and don’t know how to use technological devices, as a result, Sri Lankan government introduced compulsory subject called “General ICT” for every government school to increase technical knowledge of the students.
