The Digital Divide

Geographical Factor (Cont.)


Locally

According to the income level of the families’ people live in different places as an example most of the rich people live in urban areas this way they gain more benefits as an example they can have a high-speed internet connection which may can’t afford by the people who live in other places in the same provincial. Quality of the ADSL internet connection diminishes when the length of the line becomes long, so people who live in urban countryside places can’t use this service. sri Lankan government provides free Wi-Fi (wireless technology) in many public places library’s, bus stations likewise but sad to say a lot of people who live in far away from town areas are not using this service because they visit those places rarely.

Most of the places in the country covered with mountains, villages, rivers, etc. ability to receive television signals is low in these places. Thus they used satellite television connections by paying a bill to watch the same channels that are watched by individuals in urban areas.

Lack of electronic shops for the people who live in far away from the town areas so this way they can’t purchase electronic equipment quickly as people who live in town areas. As an example, if an individual wants to purchase a mobile phone he/she has to come more distance, so it wastes the person’s time and money due to long travel.

Students who live in a poor area can’t have a good understanding of the technology compared to the rich places so this can affect their professional life because employers these days looking for people who have good experienced and computer knowledge because most of the time organizations have to work with the computers in order to complete their relevant work. however, this is not a problem for the student who is in an urban area because they have good technical knowledge as well as experienced because most of the time they work with digital devices.
